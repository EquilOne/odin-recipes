Artisan Bread

Transform simple ingredients into a golden, crusty loaf with our easy artisan bread recipe, where a long, slow rise yields a complex flavor and a perfectly airy crumb.

This recipe invites you to experience the pure joy of homemade baking, proving that exceptional bread doesn't require complex techniques. With just a few basic ingredients and a touch of patience, you'll create a rustic loaf that boasts a satisfyingly crisp crust and a soft, flavorful interior. Whether enjoyed warm with butter, used for gourmet sandwiches, or paired with your favorite soup, this artisan bread elevates any meal, bringing the simple pleasure of freshly baked goodness to your table.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups (375g) all-purpose flour (or bread flour for a chewier crust)
  • 1.5 teaspoons (9g) salt
  • 0.5 teaspoon (2g) instant dry yeast (or active dry yeast, proofed)
  • 1.5 cups (355ml) lukewarm water

Equipment

  • Large bowl
  • Plastic wrap or a damp towel
  • Dutch oven or baking stone with a roasting pan
  • Parchment paper

Instructions

  1. Combine Dry Ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour and salt. Add the instant dry yeast and stir to combine.
  2. Add Water: Pour the lukewarm water into the flour mixture. Using a spatula or your hand, mix until a shaggy dough forms. Do not knead. Just ensure all the dry ingredients are hydrated.
  3. First Rise (Bulk Fermentation): Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a damp towel. Let the dough rest at room temperature for 12-18 hours. The longer the rise, the more complex the flavor. The dough should roughly double in size and have bubbles on the surface.
  4. Shape the Dough: Lightly flour a clean surface. Gently turn the dough out onto the floured surface. The dough will be sticky. Lightly flour the top of the dough. Fold the edges of the dough toward the center, forming a rough ball. Flip the dough over so the seam is down.
  5. Second Rise (Proofing): Line a bowl with a clean kitchen towel or parchment paper and dust it generously with flour (or a mix of flour and rice flour). Gently place the dough into the prepared bowl, seam side up. Cover with the edges of the towel or loosely with plastic wrap. Let it rise for 30-60 minutes, or until it has noticeably increased in volume.
  6. Preheat Oven and Dutch Oven: About 30 minutes before baking, preheat your oven to 450°F (232°C). If using a Dutch oven, place it in the oven to preheat as well. If using a baking stone, place the stone in the oven and place a roasting pan on the rack below.
  7. Score the Dough: Carefully remove the hot Dutch oven from the oven. If using a baking stone, have your parchment paper ready on a flat surface. Gently invert the dough onto a piece of parchment paper or into the hot Dutch oven. Use a sharp knife or a lame to score the top of the dough. Scoring helps control the bread's expansion and creates a beautiful pattern. A simple "X" or a single slash works well.
  8. Bake:
    • Dutch Oven: Cover the Dutch oven with its lid and return it to the oven. Bake for 30 minutes with the lid on. Remove the lid and bake for another 15-20 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 205-210°F (96-99°C).
    • Baking stone: Slide the parchment paper with the bread onto the preheated stone. Pour a cup of hot water into the roasting pan below to create steam. Bake for 30 minutes. Remove the roasting pan. Continue to bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 205-210°F (96-99°C).
  9. Cool: Remove the bread from the Dutch oven or baking stone and place it on a wire rack to cool completely before slicing. This allows the crumb to set.
