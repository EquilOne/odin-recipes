Traditional Sicilian Gnocchi

Savor the taste of Sicily with our traditional gnocchi recipe, where pillowy potato dumplings melt in your mouth, carrying the rich flavors of a classic Sicilian tomato sauce.

This recipe invites you to experience the heart of Sicilian cuisine, where simple ingredients are transformed into culinary delights. Our traditional gnocchi, made with tender potatoes and a touch of flour, are delicately shaped and cooked to perfection. Paired with a vibrant tomato sauce, simmered with fragrant herbs and a hint of sweetness, this dish embodies the warmth and rustic charm of Sicilian home cooking. Each bite is a journey to sun-drenched islands, where family recipes are passed down through generations, bringing comfort and joy to every meal.

Ingredients

  • For the Gnocchi:
    • 2 pounds (about 900g) russet potatoes
    • 1 large egg
    • 1 teaspoon salt
    • Approximately 2-3 cups (250-375g) all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • For the Sauce:
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 28 ounces (800g) crushed tomatoes
    • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
    • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
    • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
    • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish (optional)
    • Grated Pecorino Romano cheese, for serving (optional)

Equipment

  • Large pot
  • Potato ricer or masher
  • Large mixing bowl
  • Clean work surface
  • Knife
  • Gnocchi board or fork (optional)
  • Large saucepan
  • Slotted spoon

Instructions

  1. Cook the Potatoes: Place the potatoes in a large pot of cold, salted water. Bring to a boil and cook until tender when pierced with a fork, about 25-30 minutes. Drain and let them cool slightly.
  2. Peel and Mash: Once cool enough to handle, peel the potatoes and pass them through a potato ricer or mash them thoroughly until smooth.
  3. Form the Dough: On a clean surface, mound the mashed potatoes. Make a well in the center and add the egg and salt. Gradually add the flour, mixing with your hands until a soft, slightly sticky dough forms. Do not overwork the dough.
  4. Shape the Gnocchi: Divide the dough into smaller portions. Roll each portion into a long rope about 3/4 inch thick. Cut the rope into 3/4 inch pieces. If desired, gently roll each piece over a gnocchi board or the back of a fork to create ridges.
  5. Prepare the Sauce: While you shape the gnocchi, heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  6. Simmer the Sauce: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, sugar, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat and cook for at least 30 minutes, or longer for a richer flavor, stirring occasionally.
  7. Cook the Gnocchi: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Drop the gnocchi into the boiling water in batches. They will float to the surface when cooked, about 2-3 minutes. Remove them with a slotted spoon and add them directly to the sauce.
  8. Serve: Gently toss the gnocchi with the sauce. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves and grated Pecorino Romano cheese, if desired.
